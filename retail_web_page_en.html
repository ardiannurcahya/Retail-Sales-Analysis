Retail Customer Data Analysis Report

1. Introduction

1.1 Background

In the retail industry, understanding customer purchasing patterns is key to increasing the effectiveness of marketing and sales strategies. By analyzing customer data in depth, companies can identify consumer behavior, segment markets, and design more targeted strategies to increase customer loyalty and business profitability.

One approach used in customer data analysis is the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) method, which helps classify customers based on when they last made a purchase (Recency), how often they transact (Frequency), and the total value of their transactions (Monetary). In addition, clustering techniques can be applied to group customers based on similarities in their purchasing patterns, so that companies can develop more personalized marketing strategies. Furthermore, the calculation of Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) allows companies to predict long-term customer value, helping in decision making regarding marketing investments and customer retention.

In this project, retail sales data analysis is carried out by applying the RFM, clustering, and Customer Lifetime Value methods to identify customer segmentation and optimize business strategies. The results of data processing are stored in a SQL database to ensure data efficiency and regularity, and are integrated with Tableau to facilitate data visualization and interpretation. With this approach, companies can gain deeper insights into customer behavior and make data-driven decisions to improve business competitiveness.

1.2 Analysis Objectives

  • Identify customer purchasing patterns.
  • Segment customers based on transaction data.
  • Create an Artificial Neural Network (ANN) model to predict churn.
  • Create a dashboard for real-time sales monitoring

2. Data and Methods

2.1 Data Source

The dataset used was obtained from a retail database that records customer transactions. This data consists of 3 csv files:

Customer-Details.csv

This dataset contains customer information, including their demographic and transaction data.

  • City, State, Country: Customer location.
  • Age, Gender: Customer demographic information.
  • Income: Customer income.
  • Customer_Segment: Customer segment.
  • Date: Transaction date.
  • Total_Purchases, Amount: Transaction amount and value.
  • Shipping_Method, Payment_Method: Shipping and payment methods.
  • Ratings: Customer ratings.
  • Product_Category, Product_Type, Products: Information about the products purchased.

Order-Details.csv

Dataset This records the transaction information made by the customer.

  • Transaction_ID: Unique transaction ID.
  • Customer_ID: Customer ID.
  • Date: Transaction date.
  • Total_Purchases, Amount: Number of items and total transaction price.
  • Product_Id: Product ID.
  • Feedback: Customer feedback.
  • Shipping_Method, Payment_Method: Shipping method and payment.
  • Order_Status: Order status.
  • Ratings: Customer ratings of the transaction.

Product-Details.csv

This dataset contains information about available products.

  • Product_Id: Unique product ID.
  • Product_Category: Product category.
  • Product_Type: Product type.
  • Products: Product name.

    3. Results and Findings

    3.1 Purchase Patterns

    Based on data analysis, the product most purchased by customers is water, while the product with the least number of purchases is bluestarAC. When viewed from the total purchases, male customers have a higher transaction value compared to female customers. In terms of product categories, electronics and clothing are the most frequently purchased categories, while the category with the largest total transaction value (amount) is electronics. Geographically, the USA has the highest total transactions, followed by the UK, while at the city level, Chicago recorded the largest number of transactions. In addition, based on customer information, regular customers with medium income are the group with the highest total transactions.

Customers who have not made a purchase in the last 6 months have an 80% chance of churn. In addition, there are general factors such as total amount and age that have an influence on churn.     • 4. Conclusion and Recommendations

    4.1 Conclusion

    Sales analysis shows that the product with the highest number of purchases is Water, with a total of 128 thousand transactions. The overall sales trend tends to be stagnant without any significant increase or decrease. In terms of product categories, Electronics recorded the highest transaction value (amount). In customer analysis, the Recency, Frequency, Monetary (RFM) method identifies that customers with the highest Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) are the most valuable customers for the business. The top ten customers have the highest transaction value, making them the primary target in customer retention strategies. Meanwhile, in terms of location, the country with the largest total transactions is the United States (US), while the city with the highest transactions was recorded in Chicago.

    4.2 Recommendations

    • Weekday Promos: Offer discounts or loyalty programs on weekdays to increase sales.
    • Churn Retention Programs: Send emails or notifications with special offers to customers at risk of churn.
    • Differentiation Strategy: Adjust marketing strategies based on customer segmentation, for example providing exclusive promos to high spenders.
    • Best Customers: Provide loyalty programs to keep them active.
    • Big Spenders: Upsell and cross-sell premium products.
    • New Customers: Drive more transactions with special new customer discounts.
    • Churn Risk Customers: Send special offers or retention emails to encourage them to shop again.
